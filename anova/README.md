# lc_anova — LC-PINN × ANOVA interpretability

Functional ANOVA decomposition of the joint $(x, \lambda)$ input space of
trained loss-conditional PINNs. CIKM 2026 submission target. Headline finding:
on 2D Helmholtz the triplet $S_{x, y, k} \approx 0.43$ dominates the variance
decomposition, confirmed across 60+ independent computations and 50 HDMR
training seeds.

## Reproducing the headline 2D Helmholtz result

```bash

  --checkpoint /Users/anna/Desktop/research/thesis/code/checkpoints/lc_pinn_helmholtz_2d_seed0_film_lbfgs_w64.pt \
  --max-order 3 --fourier --hidden 128 --layers 3 --num-freqs 6 \
```

## Validation

- **Synthetic polynomial** with closed-form analytic ANOVA: recovers all Sobol indices to within 0.005 absolute, both tanh order-2 and Fourier order-3 cases. Critically, Fourier order-3 returns triplet ≈ 0.0008 on the synthetic (no hallucinated triplet).
- **MC-Sobol estimator** validated against analytic Sobol on synthetic to within 0.005.
- **Proposition 1** (closed-form $S^{(k)}_{x, y}$ for the manufactured Helmholtz solution) matches MC measurement on the trained LC-PINN to within 0.029 absolute at every $k$.

## Headline finding — narrative

The dominant ANOVA subset for 2D Helmholtz parametric solver is the
irreducible $(x, y, k)$ triplet. This finding is:

1. **Convergent across 4 independent computations**: analytic ref via MC-Sobol,
   LC-PINN seed-0 MC-Sobol, LC-PINN seed-2 MC-Sobol, and Fourier HDMR — all
   within 2 pp.
2. **Robust across LC-PINN training seeds**: std < 0.001 at $N = 2 \times 10^5$
   MC samples.
3. **Robust across HDMR-RNG training seeds**: 50-run distribution has
   std 0.019 with mean 0.560 (HDMR-normalised) — 95 % CI $[0.522, 0.592]$.
4. **Backed by closed-form analysis** (Prop. 1) for the conditional spatial
   Sobol curve at any fixed $k$.
5. **Inaccessible to standard interpretability tools**: Integrated Gradients
   and gradient-magnitude attribution methods produce only first-order
   per-input importance; the triplet has no analog in those frameworks.
