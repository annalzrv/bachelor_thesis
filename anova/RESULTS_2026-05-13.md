### 4. LC-PINN application — `lc_anova/helmholtz_pipeline.py`

Joint $(x, y, k)$ HDMR applied to a trained 2D Helmholtz FiLM+L-BFGS LC-PINN
checkpoint (seed 0). 30k joint samples, 4 ablation runs:

| Variant | Val rel-RMSE | Captured var | Triplet Sobol (normalised) |
|---|---|---|---|
| tanh, hidden=32, order-2 | 0.7326 | 46% | — (no triplet term) |
| tanh, hidden=128 layers=3, order-3 | 0.6654 | 56% | 0.053 |
| Fourier L=4, hidden=64, order-3 | 0.6591 | 57% | 0.563 |
| **Fourier L=6, hidden=128, order-3, 400 ep** | **0.4526** | **80%** | **0.567** |

The **Fourier features + capacity bump dramatically improve reconstruction**.
Without Fourier features, tanh MLPs cannot represent $\cos(kx)$ at $k \in [1, 10]$.

**Robust finding across four independent methods**: the $(x, y, k)$ triplet
carries 42–46% of the LC-PINN output variance — the dominant single subset
contribution. This is intrinsic to the multiplicative structure of the
analytic solution $u_\text{ref} = \sin(\pi x) \sin(\pi y) \cos(kx) \cos(ky)$,
not a network artifact.

## Implications for the CIKM paper

The L4 narrative (amortised Sobol sensitivity analysis as primary, ANOVA
signatures as secondary) is well-supported:

1. **MC-Sobol on LC-PINN** is the primary claim: compute Sobol indices for
   a parametric PDE family at $O(1)$ compute per parameter value (vs. $O(N)$
   for per-$\lambda$ retraining). Across-seed reproducibility within 0.001.
2. **Fourier-feature joint HDMR** is the methodological contribution:
   produces functional signatures $u_x, u_\lambda, u_{x,\lambda}$ plus a
   triplet $u_{x,y,\lambda}$ that no order-2 method can recover.
   Cross-validates against MC-Sobol within ~1pp in absolute-variance terms.
3. **The triplet finding generalises**: the same triplet-dominant structure
   holds on the analytic reference AND across LC-PINN training seeds.

### What's still untested (Day 3+)

- **Burgers LC-PINN** — apply the same pipeline to confirm method generality.
- **Per-$k$ Sobol indices** — current MC-Sobol averages over the $k$ range;
  we may want $S_i(k)$ as a function of $k$ to give a richer signature.
- **Compute cost claim** — actually time the MC-Sobol vs per-$k$-retrained
  pipeline to get the "1% compute cost" number.
- **Failure-mode localisation** — at large $k$ near the boundary, does the
  pair-effect heatmap localise pathologies that scalar accuracy metrics miss?
- **Signature visualisation** — render publication-quality $u_x(x,y)$,
  $u_\lambda(k)$, $u_{x,k}(x,k)$, $u_{y,k}(y,k)$ plots from the trained HDMR.
